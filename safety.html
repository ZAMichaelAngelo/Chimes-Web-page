Understanding Crane Operation Risks

Mobile crane operations involve inherent risks that we actively manage through comprehensive safety protocols:

Our Control: Load calculations, regular NDT testing, strict load chart adherence, and pre-lift inspections.

‚ö†Ô∏è Crane Overturning

Risk: Unstable ground, incorrect outrigger setup, or excessive radius.

‚ö†Ô∏è Contact or Collision

Risk: Overhead power lines, structures, or personnel in the lift zone.

‚ö†Ô∏è Falling Objects

Our Safety Protocols

Every project follows our comprehensive safety framework from planning to execution:

1. Pre-Job Planning

Site assessment, hazard identification, risk analysis, and engineered lift plan development with CAD modeling.

2. Safety Briefings

Toolbox talks, role assignments, emergency procedures, and communication protocols for all personnel.

3. Equipment Inspection

Daily pre-operational checks, load chart verification, rigging inspection, and system functionality tests.

4. Operational Monitoring

Continuous supervision, real-time adjustments, weather monitoring, and strict adherence to lift plans.

5. Post-Job Review

Incident reporting, lessons learned documentation, and continuous improvement implementation.
